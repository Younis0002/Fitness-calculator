Your Fitness Metrics

Body Mass Index (BMI)

What is BMI? BMI is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It helps categorize individuals as underweight (<18.5), normal (18.5-24.9), overweight (25-29.9), or obese (≥30). It’s a general guide and doesn’t account for muscle mass or body composition.

Daily Calorie Needs

What are Calorie Needs? This is the number of calories your body needs daily to maintain, gain, or lose weight, based on your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and activity level. For muscle gain, a surplus is added; for weight loss, a deficit is applied.
Sources: Calorie-rich foods include nuts (e.g., almonds, 576 kcal/100g; cashews, 553 kcal/100g), peanut butter (588 kcal/100g), dates (277 kcal/100g), and paratha (325 kcal/100g). Alternatives: If unavailable, use whole grains (e.g., oats, 389 kcal/100g), bread (265 kcal/100g), or bananas (89 kcal/100g).

Protein Intake

What is Protein Intake? Protein is essential for muscle repair and growth. Recommended intake varies by goal: higher for muscle gain (1.6-2.2g/kg body weight) and moderate for weight loss (1.2-1.8g/kg) to preserve muscle mass.
Sources: High-protein foods include chicken breast (31g/100g), paneer (20g/100g), eggs (13g/100g), and daal chana (9g/100g). Alternatives: If unavailable, use lentils (e.g., daal masoor, 9g/100g), chickpeas (9g/100g), or fish (22g/100g).

Carbohydrate Intake

What are Carbs? Carbohydrates provide energy for daily activities and exercise. Muscle gain diets may include 4-7g/kg body weight, while weight loss diets may lower carbs (2-5g/kg) to create a calorie deficit.
Sources: High-carb foods include roti (40g/100g), boiled rice (28g/100g), potatoes (17g/100g), and bananas (23g/100g). Alternatives: If unavailable, use bread (49g/100g), corn (19g/100g), or mangoes (15g/100g).

Fat Intake

What is Dietary Fat? Fats support hormone production and overall health. Aim for 0.5-1g/kg body weight for both goals, adjusted to fit calorie needs.
Sources: Healthy fats include avocados (15g/100g), paneer (20g/100g), peanuts (49g/100g), and paratha with ghee (12g/100g). Alternatives: If unavailable, use cashews (44g/100g), walnuts (65g/100g), or yogurt (3.3g/100g).
